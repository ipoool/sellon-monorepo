# SellOn — Brand Voice Guide

Use this file to inform all copywriting, captions, ad copy, scripts, and any text output produced for SellOn.

---

## Tone of Voice

SellOn speaks like a fellow seller who's been around longer — not an expensive consultant, not a big corporate brand. Warm, encouraging, and honest about the everyday challenges Indonesian SMEs face.

| Dimension | Description |
|---|---|
| **Warm** | Speaks like a friend, not a company. Empathy first, solution second. |
| **Encouraging** | UMKM sellers often feel their business is "too small" for technology. SellOn reassures them: this is for you. |
| **Professional but accessible** | No technical jargon. Never condescending. But serious about reliability and trust. |
| **Results-oriented** | Talk about real outcomes felt by sellers, not technical features. |
| **Honest** | No over-promising. No empty claims. |

## Voice Pillars

### 1. Empowering UMKM
Write as if you understand the daily reality of an UMKM seller. Acknowledge the difficulty, then offer a way out.
- "Managing orders via WA was exhausting. SellOn makes the process automatic."
- "You focus on selling. Let SellOn handle the admin."

### 2. Simplicity-First
SellOn is powerful, but don't talk about technical features. Talk about ease and results.
- Use: "Open a store in 5 minutes"
- Avoid: "Our platform integrates the RajaOngkir REST API for real-time shipping cost calculation"

### 3. Trustworthy
UMKM sellers are very selective about trust — especially for something that touches their finances.
- Always be transparent: "Payment goes directly into your account, not SellOn's"
- Avoid claims that can't be verified

### 4. Results-Oriented
UMKM sellers want proof, not promises. Show concrete, realistic impact.
- "No more replying to 'is this still in stock?' one by one"
- "All orders in one place — nothing gets missed"

## Language Rules

### Always
- Start with the seller's problem or benefit, not a product feature
- Use "you" / "your" to address sellers directly
- Use active voice: "SellOn sends you an order notification" not "An order notification will be sent"
- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Natural language — not stiff, not too formal, not too slang
- Clear, actionable CTAs: "Try Free", "Create Your Store Now", "See Demo"

### Never
- Use technical jargon: "API", "REST", "webhook", "real-time database sync", etc. without explanation
- Use corporate speak: "leverage", "comprehensive solution", "integrated ecosystem"
- Talk down to sellers or dismiss the way they currently work
- Over-promise: avoid "reach billions in revenue" or unverifiable claims
- Write about SellOn in third person in social copy ("SellOn enables users to...")

## Writing Mechanics

### Headlines
- Max 10 words for social, max 15 for ads
- Lead with a problem, benefit, or relevant number
- Examples:
  - "WA orders a mess? Here's the fix."
  - "Open an online store in 5 minutes."
  - "Accept orders 24/7 without being on call."

### Captions (Social Media)
- First line is the hook — make people stop scrolling
- Body: 3–5 short lines, blank line between paragraphs
- CTA on the last line
- Hashtags: 5–10, relevant, mix of popular and niche UMKM tags
- Structure:
  ```
  [Hook — 1 line]

  [Problem or context — 1–2 lines]

  [SellOn solution / benefit — 1–2 lines]

  [CTA — 1 line]

  #hashtag #hashtag #hashtag
  ```

### Ad Copy
- **Primary text**: Hook (1 line) + Problem (1 line) + SellOn solution (1 line) + CTA (1 line)
- **Headline**: Benefit-first, max 6 words
- **Description**: Supporting detail, one clear value prop
- Always prepare at least 3 headline variants for testing

### Scripts (Voiceover / Video)
- Write as spoken language — contractions, short sentences, natural pauses
- Mark pauses with `[pause]` or `—`
- Label segments: `[HOOK]`, `[PROBLEM]`, `[SOLUTION]`, `[CTA]`

## Vocabulary

### Use These Words
online store, orders, inventory, buyer, seller, SME, organized, automated, easy, fast, instant, professional, tracked, notification, dashboard, catalog, checkout

### Avoid These Words
leverage, utilize, comprehensive solution, platform (as a verb), scalable, robust, seamlessly, cutting-edge, revolutionary, synergy

## Tone by Platform

| Platform | Tone Adjustment |
|---|---|
| **Instagram Feed** | Slightly more polished. Strong visual hook. Informative but still concise. |
| **Instagram Story** | Casual and interactive. Questions, polls, tap/swipe CTAs. Max 6 words per text element. |
| **Instagram Reels** | Hook in first 3 seconds. Fast narrative. Show pain → solution → result. |
| **TikTok** | Most casual. POV framing works well. Relatable or controversial hook. Feels native, not like an ad. |
| **WhatsApp Broadcast** | Warm and personal, like a message from a friend. Not formal. One message, one CTA. |
| **Facebook** | More context. Relatable mini seller story. Can be longer. |
| **Email** | Conversational opener, clear value, one CTA. No walls of text. |

## Copy Examples

### Good
> "How many WA orders did you miss last week because you forgot or got confused? With SellOn, every order goes to one dashboard — nothing gets missed."

> "Buyers can check stock, pick a product, and pay on their own. You don't need to be on call 24/7."

> "Money goes straight into your account. SellOn doesn't hold a single rupiah of yours."

> "New store set up in 5 minutes. Share the link in your WA group. Start receiving orders."

### Bad
> "SellOn leverages cutting-edge technology to deliver a comprehensive e-commerce solution for Indonesian SME players within an integrated digital ecosystem."

> "Our platform seamlessly integrates payment gateway and shipping APIs for an optimal shopping experience."

> "Reach billions in revenue just by joining SellOn!"
